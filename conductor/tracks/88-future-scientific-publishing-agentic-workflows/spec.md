# Track 88 - Future Scientific Publishing and Agentic Editorial Workflows

## Goal

Incorporate the strategic research on reimagining the scientific paper into the Sourceright roadmap, with explicit coverage of agentic submission management, editorial workflow automation, and post-publication monitoring.

## User outcome

A journal or publisher can use Sourceright as part of a broader validation infrastructure for future research objects, not only as a citation screening utility. The roadmap should show how current report artifacts can grow into structured, auditable submission packages that support living papers, executable research objects, reviewed preprints, claim graphs, and post-publication maintenance.

## Strategic frame

The future paper is treated as a submission, validation, review, publication, and maintenance object. A journal should be able to receive a structured research package containing article text, claims, data, code, ethics metadata, reporting checklists, author contributions, conflicts, AI-use declarations, peer-review history, persistent identifiers, and links to all associated research objects.

Sourceright's near-term role is to validate the citation, source, provenance, recency, and reporting-integrity parts of that package. The long-term role is to expose those checks as auditable workflow services that can be used by editors, reviewers, authors, and post-publication monitors.

## Scope

- Preserve the five future paper options as roadmap context:
  - living evidence records;
  - executable research objects;
  - refereed preprint network papers;
  - machine-actionable claim graphs;
  - community-governed research ledgers.
- Add agentic submission-management stages to the journal workflow surface:
  - pre-submission readiness checks;
  - structured submission intake;
  - automated triage and routing;
  - reviewer selection support;
  - technical validation and integrity checks;
  - reviewer and editor briefing packs;
  - structured decision support;
  - post-publication monitoring.
- Map Sourceright capabilities to each stage without claiming autonomous editorial decision-making.
- Keep the output compatible with Track 16 journal workflow integrations and Track 87 self-improving registry work.
- Define evidence, audit, and approval boundaries for any agentic workflow.

## Out of scope

- Automated accept, reject, or clinical-use decisions.
- Autonomous publication of support claims.
- Uploading confidential manuscripts to third-party AI systems without explicit policy and permission.
- Replacing editors or reviewers with agents.
- Treating preprints as clinically actionable guidance without clear status signalling.

## Required infrastructure

- Structured article and metadata intake.
- Persistent identifiers for articles, datasets, code, authors, funders, reviews, versions, and protocols.
- Machine-readable reporting checklists.
- Versioned validation reports and audit logs.
- Platform adapters for OJS, Janeway, enterprise manuscript systems, and generic webhook workflows.
- Optional executable environments for computational submissions.
- Post-publication monitoring hooks for corrections, retractions, broken links, new evidence, and public commentary.

## Scientific rigor mechanisms

- Human editorial authority over all recommendations and decisions.
- Explicit status labels for preprints, reviewed preprints, versions of record, living updates, corrections, and retractions.
- Reviewer and editor audit trails for agentic recommendations.
- Separate evidence extraction, agent synthesis, and human decision records.
- Reproducibility checks for executable research objects.
- Claim-level provenance for machine-actionable records.
- Update triggers and public changelogs for living evidence records.
- Bias, manipulation, confidentiality, and robustness checks for reviewer matching and triage agents.

## Claim boundary

> Agents may structure, check, route, summarize, and monitor. Editors remain accountable for editorial decisions, reviewers remain accountable for reviews, and authors remain accountable for submitted content.

## Evidence level target

**roadmap** - the track is complete when the strategic model, workflow stages, rigor boundaries, and transition plan are explicit enough to guide later implementation tracks.
