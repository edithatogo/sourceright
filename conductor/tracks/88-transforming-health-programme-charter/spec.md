# Track 88 - Transforming Health Programme Charter

## Goal

Define the Transforming Health publishing project as a transformative, open-source, agent-first scholarly communication programme focused on improving health and related social outcomes.

## User outcome

A journal, funder, or platform maintainer can understand the proposed programme, what it changes, what it does not yet claim, and how it creates value beyond a static article.

## Scope

- Define the journal concept and naming options: Transforming Health, Transforming Knowledge, and Transforming Science.
- Recommend Transforming Health as the initial topic because it can support cross-disciplinary work across clinical care, public health, social outcomes, health economics, implementation, policy, data science, AI, and engineering.
- Define the platform as open source and self-improving.
- Recognize contributions to the platform itself as scholarly and technical contributions.
- Establish that each research project can produce many outputs from one knowledge package.
- Define how the platform should reduce pressure for salami-slicing by increasing value from a single structured project.
- Define contribution categories for developers, data scientists, economists, epidemiologists, engineers, biostatisticians, bioinformaticians, clinicians, editors, reviewers, translators, and community contributors.

## Out of scope

- Launching a live journal.
- Making clinical recommendations from unreviewed submissions.
- Choosing a final name without stakeholder and trademark review.
- Defining payment, compensation, or token economics as implementation-ready policy.

## Contract boundary

The charter is a programme contract. It does not create platform support or editorial policy by itself. All support claims must be backed by later tracks, fixtures, tests, and governance decisions.

## Completion criteria

- Programme purpose is documented.
- Transforming Health is recorded as the recommended initial focus.
- Open-source self-improvement and contribution-credit principles are documented.
- Dependencies to package, workflow, governance, presentation, and economics tracks are explicit.
