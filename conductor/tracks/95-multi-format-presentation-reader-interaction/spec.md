# Track 95 - Multi-Format Presentation and Reader Interaction

## Goal

Generate multiple reader-facing outputs from the same verified research package, and allow readers to choose their own entry point through controlled retrieval and summarization.

## User outcome

A reader can interact with a research project as a preprint, blog summary, slide deck, audio script, video script, short-form explanation, long-form article, monograph-like expansion, or chat interface, while the underlying evidence and status labels remain consistent.

## Scope

- Generate or curate preprints, blog posts, presentations, audio scripts, video scripts, short-form summaries, long-form articles, monographs, graphical abstracts, policy briefs, and community summaries.
- Support a chat-with-article interface over the research package, not over an unbounded web search.
- Allow readers to declare role, interest, positionality, language, expertise, and preferred format.
- Keep generated outputs linked to package nodes, source evidence, reviews, limitations, and status labels.
- Record external dissemination outputs such as Substack, blog posts, press releases, news media, podcasts, videos, social media, and conference materials as graph nodes.

## Out of scope

- Presenting generated summaries as new evidence.
- Removing clinical, policy, preprint, or review status labels from generated outputs.
- Generating personalized medical advice.

## Completion criteria

- Presentation surface contract is documented.
- Reader profile inputs are documented.
- Evidence-bound RAG and citation boundaries are documented.
- Generated outputs remain linked to the research package graph.
