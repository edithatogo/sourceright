# Track 97 - Governance, Identity, Transparency, and Anti-Gaming

## Goal

Define governance for editorial AI, human oversight, identity, conflicts, privacy, transparency, incentives, audit logs, and abuse prevention.

## User outcome

The platform can use agents and contribution incentives without hiding accountability, weakening peer review, leaking confidential manuscripts, or creating new ways to game scholarly credit.

## Scope

- Define role-specific AI policy for authors, reviewers, editors, production staff, platform agents, and readers.
- Require disclosure for agent-generated or agent-assisted review, summaries, translations, and recommendations.
- Require audit logs for all agentic actions.
- Define identity and profile-linking policy.
- Define conflict-of-interest capture, verification, and appeal processes.
- Define privacy and confidentiality boundaries for manuscripts, data, reviews, and contributor profiles.
- Define anti-gaming controls for reciprocal review, contribution inflation, review rings, AI-generated low-value contributions, citation manipulation, hidden prompts, and conflict evasion.
- Define bias and robustness audits for reviewer matching, triage, review synthesis, contribution scoring, and recommendation systems.

## Out of scope

- Fully automated final editorial authority.
- Opaque reputation scores.
- Unconsented use of private social or profile data.
- Treating agent outputs as human outputs.

## Completion criteria

- Editorial AI governance requirements are documented.
- Identity and profile-linking boundaries are documented.
- Anti-gaming controls are documented.
- Audit and appeal pathways are documented.
- Human authority remains the default for editorial decisions.
