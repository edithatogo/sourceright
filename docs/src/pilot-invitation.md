# Pilot Invitation

Sourceright is ready for technical-preview pilots with users who understand that
the tool is a structured triage assistant, not a final examiner-grade verifier.

## Good pilot fits

- Authors preparing a manuscript reference list before submission.
- Editors or reviewers doing first-pass citation hygiene checks.
- Repository or library teams evaluating metadata provenance workflows.
- Legal or policy users who need separate citation extraction without mixing it
  into academic CSL.

## Pilot scope

A useful pilot should test a small but realistic workspace, including canonical
CSL, sidecar evidence, review queue output, reports, and export previews. Live
provider checks should be treated as opt-in evidence gathering.

## What feedback should capture

- Whether diagnostics are clear enough to act on.
- Whether review queues reduce manual triage time.
- Whether provider evidence and canonical CSL remain understandable.
- Whether export previews are low-risk for downstream tools.
- Where DOCX/PDF extraction, URL checking, archive checks, or writeback
  suggestions need stronger handling.

## What is out of scope

Do not ask pilot users to treat Sourceright as a production institutional
platform, a legal filing compliance system, an AI detector, or a public
benchmarking claim.
